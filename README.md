# CESES Workspace

CESES means Clean Energy & Smart Energy Solutions in Cambodia.

This repository is the working space for building the company step by step. It keeps strategy, customer validation, market research, business models, proposals, operations, and finance documents in one organized place.

The goal is simple: help the founder make clear decisions, test real customer demand, and prepare CESES for partnerships, pilots, and funding.

## Focus Areas

- Solar solutions for SMEs, factories, farms, schools, and commercial buildings
- EV charging opportunities in Cambodia
- Technical capacity building for clean energy workers and partners
- Market research on customers, competitors, policy, and pricing
- Business models, budgets, pitch materials, and operating procedures

## Folder Guide

- `01-strategy-roadmap`: Company direction, priorities, and milestone planning
- `02-founder-validation`: Founder assumptions, customer interviews, and early proof
- `03-solar-sme`: Solar products, target customers, partners, and pilot ideas
- `04-ev-charging`: EV charging business ideas, sites, economics, and partners
- `05-technical-capacity`: Training, hiring, technical standards, and installer capability
- `06-market-research`: Cambodia market data, policy, competitors, and customer segments
- `07-business-model`: Revenue models, pricing, unit economics, and go-to-market plans
- `08-proposals-pitch-deck`: Investor, partner, donor, and customer proposal materials
- `09-operations-sop`: Daily operating procedures, templates, checklists, and quality control
- `10-finance-budget`: Budgets, forecasts, funding needs, and financial tracking
- `99-codex-experiments`: Drafts, AI experiments, rough ideas, and temporary exploration

## How To Use This Workspace

1. Put each document in the folder where it best belongs.
2. Keep file names clear, such as `customer-interview-notes.md` or `solar-sme-pricing.xlsx`.
3. Use `99-codex-experiments` for rough drafts before moving useful work into the main folders.
4. Review the roadmap and finance folders regularly so decisions stay connected to real priorities and budget.
